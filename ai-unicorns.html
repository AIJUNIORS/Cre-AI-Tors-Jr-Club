🎯 The Mission of creAItors Club — Reaffirmed at Level 4

Our goal is not to make children into career-ready AI workers. Our goal is to develop their awareness of the AI world they are growing up in, their safe and critical understanding of how AI works and fails, and their confidence to participate responsibly as citizens in an AI-shaped world. Level 4 is the culmination of that mission. Wonder, not anxiety. Agency, not compliance. Joy, not performance.

Tutor Notes - Known Corrections for This Level
C8 - Week 45 AI Citizenship not careers
Frame as: What role do you want to play in how AI develops? Not job preparation. A civic commitment.
C9 - ML concept as destination
The investigation or build activity is the vehicle. AI awareness and critical thinking is the destination. Always name it explicitly.
Core Principles - Apply at Every Level
Creators not consumers - every lesson involves making something
Safety first - cybersecurity and bias woven in always
Joy is non-negotiable - if it is not fun, something is wrong
Ethics every week - embedded question, not standalone unit
AI is human - every algorithm connects to the person who built it
Not a career pipeline - awareness, not employment preparation
Readiness not age - levels are competency-based
Mistakes are the method - debugging and failure are curriculum

Unicorns Placement Assessment
Tests Minecrafters concepts. Concept-intensive, not curriculum-intensive. Any child can take it at any time - no tutor needed. Output is a placement recommendation, never a score or grade.
?
How to use: Read questions aloud or let child read independently. When done, match answers to the Placement Guide below. Output is always a recommendation - never a score.
Q1. What is training data? Why does it matter for AI?
ML Core Concept
Looking for: examples used to teach AI what to recognise or predict
Q2. If an AI only learned from pictures of cats, what would happen when it saw a dog?
Bias and Generalisation
Looking for: it would get confused, get it wrong, or try to call it a cat
Q3. Have you ever used Teachable Machine or ML4Kids? What did you make?
Tool Experience
Any yes with description = strong signal for Unicorns entry
Q4. What does it mean to design something for a real person - not just yourself?
Human-Centred Design
Looking for: understanding other people needs, testing with real users
Q5. If AI makes a mistake that hurts someone, whose responsibility is it?
AI Ethics
Any thoughtful reasoned answer is valid - designer, company, user, all three
Q6. Name one way AI helps people and one way it has caused harm.
Balanced AI Awareness
Both parts required - awareness that AI is neither all good nor all bad
Q7. What does fairness mean when AI is making decisions?
Bias and Fairness
Looking for: everyone treated the same, data represents everyone, no group left out
Q8. If you could build one AI tool for your school or community, what would it do and who would it help?
Creative + Ethical Application
Looking for imagination + awareness of who benefits
Placement Guide
6-8 confident answers - Ready for Unicorns - begin at Week 1.
3-5 confident answers - Start Unicorns with support, or complete Minecrafters Unit 4 first.
0-2 confident answers - Complete Minecrafters first. AI tool experience is essential before Unicorns.
