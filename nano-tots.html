Academic Structure — 50 Lessons = 50 Weeks = 1 School Year

200 lessons ÷ 4 levels = 50 lessons per level. 50 lessons × 1 lesson per week = 50 weeks ≈ 1 full school year. Each week follows a 7-day workbook: Day 1 teaches ONE new concept (45–60 min), Days 2–7 provide six structured practice sessions reinforcing exactly that concept. This mirrors how rigorous academic curricula work — deep mastery of one concept per week beats shallow coverage of many. Nano Tots 🔬 = 6 and above · Pixel Blockers 🧩 = 7 and above · Minecrafters ⛏️ = 8 and above · Unicorns 🦄 = 9 and above.

🤖 Machine Learning Foundations for Ages 6–7
These 10 ML concepts are woven across all 50 weeks. Children encounter them as part of everyday learning — the ML connection is made explicit by the teacher at exactly the right moment. This is deliberate: the best way to introduce AI concepts to 6-year-olds is through activities that make complete sense at their level.

📅 7-Day Weekly Workbook Structure

🎓
Day 1 — Teach: Direct instruction. ONE new concept. Teacher-led. 45–60 min. Vocabulary introduced.
✏️
Day 2 — Guided Practice: Structured worksheets with teacher support. Reinforces Day 1 concept.
🎲
Day 3 — Unplugged: Physical hands-on activity. No technology required. Games, role-play, building.
💻
Day 4 — Digital Explore: Supervised tool exploration. Student-led with teacher available.
🎨
Day 5 — Create: Open-ended creative application. Student chooses format. Highest-order thinking.
🤝
Day 6 — Share & Reflect: Peer sharing, gallery walk, family show-and-tell. Metacognition.
🏆
Day 7 — Assess & Portfolio: Traffic-light self-assessment, quiz, portfolio entry. Informs next week.

🛡️

⭐ Excellence Framework — What Mastery Looks Like
Every lesson has an Excellence Marker — an observable behaviour showing mastery beyond task completion:
① Curiosity — asks "what if?" or "why?" unprompted  ·  ② Transfer — applies concept in a context not discussed in class  ·  ③ Explanation — teaches the concept to someone younger  ·  ④ Iteration — improves work without being told to  ·  ⑤ Ethics — considers impact on others when making decisions  ·  ⑥ Originality — creates something the teacher didn't predict  ·  ⑦ Metacognition — accurately identifies their own learning gaps

Tutor Notes - Known Corrections for This Level
C1 - ML concept must be central, not a footnote
The activity is the vehicle. AI awareness is the destination. Make the ML concept connection explicit every lesson.
C2 - Sorting/classification human connection
Weeks 13-14: always connect sorting back to AI. When AI sorts data, who decides the rules? Who could be harmed?
Core Principles - Apply at Every Level
Creators not consumers - every lesson involves making something
Safety first - cybersecurity and bias woven in always
Joy is non-negotiable - if it is not fun, something is wrong
Ethics every week - embedded question, not standalone unit
AI is human - every algorithm connects to the person who built it
Not a career pipeline - awareness, not employment preparation
Readiness not age - levels are competency-based
Mistakes are the method - debugging and failure are curriculum
