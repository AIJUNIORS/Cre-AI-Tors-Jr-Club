Minecrafters — 8 and above — 50 Weeks — First Real AI Tools

This is where AI moves from concept to experience. In Nano Tots and Pixel Blockers students learned ABOUT AI. In Minecrafters they USE it. Teachable Machine from Week 9, ML4Kids from Week 14, connected to Scratch from Week 15. Students train real models, experience bias firsthand, build a personal ethics framework, design and build an AI project using human-centred design thinking, and celebrate Canada's world-leading role in AI. All content verified against Ontario Grade 3, CSTA 3-5, MIT RAISE, and Common Sense Media Grade 3 standards.

Tutor Guide - Level 2 - Minecrafters
For tutors, educators, and parents. Summarises what children learn, what they arrive knowing, corrections to apply, and how to deliver this level well.
The Mission
"Every child deserves a strong, confident beginning with AI - because it is shaping the world they will grow into. creAItors Club provides that foundation, introducing AI early through a clear, structured approach so children can build understanding, spark curiosity, and develop the confidence to shape what comes next."
Ideal Age
8 and above
Age is a guide, not a gate
Assessment
Placement assessment available - tests Pixel Blockers concepts. See Assessment tab.

Real AI tool experience (Teachable Machine, ML4Kids). Training data, bias, fairness hands-on. Human-centred design. Personal ethics framework.

Ethics every week: Every lesson includes an embedded ethics question. The ML concept connects the activity to AI awareness. Make this connection explicit every time - it is the point of the lesson.
Tutor Notes - Known Corrections for This Level
C6 - Unit 1 safety thread absent
Weeks 1-8 are almost entirely technical. Add a reflection at Week 6 or 7: We are about to use real AI tools. Before we do - what should we think about?
C7 - Unit 3 heaviness risk
Weeks 21-30 are consecutive real-world AI problems. Every week needs one positive AI story alongside the concern. Children should leave feeling capable, not anxious.
C8 - Week 45 AI Citizenship not careers
Do not frame as career preparation. Frame as: What role do YOU want to play in how AI develops?
C9 - ML concept as central purpose
The Scratch activity or Teachable Machine session is the vehicle. AI awareness is the destination. Make this explicit every lesson.
Core Principles - Apply at Every Level
Creators not consumers - every lesson involves making something
Safety first - cybersecurity and bias woven in always
Joy is non-negotiable - if it is not fun, something is wrong
Ethics every week - embedded question, not standalone unit
AI is human - every algorithm connects to the person who built it
Not a career pipeline - awareness, not employment preparation
Readiness not age - levels are competency-based
Mistakes are the method - debugging and failure are curriculum

Minecrafters Placement Assessment
Tests Pixel Blockers concepts. Concept-intensive, not curriculum-intensive. Any child can take it at any time - no tutor needed. Output is a placement recommendation, never a score or grade.
?
How to use: Read questions aloud or let child read independently. When done, match answers to the Placement Guide below. Output is always a recommendation - never a score.
Q1. In a game, what is a score? Why does it change?
Variables - Concept
Looking for: a number that tracks something and updates when something happens
Q2. If a character touches a wall, it should bounce back. What kind of thinking is this?
Conditionals - If/Then
Looking for: if this happens, then do that - may not use technical words
Q3. What makes a game fair or unfair? Give an example.
Ethics in Design
Any thoughtful answer - tests whether the ethics thread landed
Q4. What is a loop? Give a real-life example - not from coding.
Loops - Conceptual
Accept: brushing teeth every day, seasons repeating, a song chorus
Q5. Have you ever built anything in Scratch or ScratchJr? What did you make?
Prior Experience
Yes with description = strong signal. Not required for entry.
Q6. Your code makes a character go the wrong way. What do you do first?
Debugging Approach
Looking for systematic thinking: check, test, fix - not give up or start over
Q7. What is one way an AI program is different from a normal computer program?
AI Awareness
AI learns from examples; normal programs follow fixed rules - any version of this
Q8. Why is it important to use strong passwords?
Cybersecurity Basics
Looking for: protecting personal information, preventing others from accessing accounts
Placement Guide
6-8 confident answers - Ready for Unicorns. Consider Minecrafters Week 40+ or jump to Unicorns assessment.
3-5 confident answers - Start Minecrafters. Prior experience guides starting week (typically Week 10-20).
0-2 confident answers - Start at Pixel Blockers. A strong foundation before tools is essential.
