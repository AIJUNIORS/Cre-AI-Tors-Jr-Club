Pixel Blockers — 7 and above — 50 Weeks

Builds directly on Nano Tots. Students transition from ScratchJr to full Scratch, learn game design fundamentals, deepen their logic and cybersecurity knowledge, and encounter AI concepts for the first time. 10 real pioneer stories are woven through the curriculum — one every 5 weeks — connecting computing history to the concepts being learned. AI stays conceptual only in Pixel Blockers — no tools until Minecrafters. All content verified against Ontario Grade 2, CSTA K-2, and MIT RAISE standards.

Tutor Guide - Level 1 - Pixel Blockers
For tutors, educators, and parents. Summarises what children learn, what they arrive knowing, corrections to apply, and how to deliver this level well.
The Mission
"Every child deserves a strong, confident beginning with AI - because it is shaping the world they will grow into. creAItors Club provides that foundation, introducing AI early through a clear, structured approach so children can build understanding, spark curiosity, and develop the confidence to shape what comes next."

Ethics every week: Every lesson includes an embedded ethics question. The ML concept connects the activity to AI awareness. Make this connection explicit every time - it is the point of the lesson.
Tutor Notes - Known Corrections for This Level
C3 - Unit 2 ethics thread thin
Game design weeks 11-20 are motivating but safety awareness gets thin. Treat the Day 6 ethics question as the main event - not an afterthought.
C4 - AI weeks 38-45 energy risk
Coming after 37 weeks of Scratch, energy can drop. Use pioneer stories as emotional re-entry. Make these weeks feel like the reward.
C5 - Add AI awareness bridge Week 25
At Week 25 connect current logic skills to how AI uses the same logic. Keeps the AI thread alive throughout.
Core Principles - Apply at Every Level
Creators not consumers - every lesson involves making something
Safety first - cybersecurity and bias woven in always
Joy is non-negotiable - if it is not fun, something is wrong
Ethics every week - embedded question, not standalone unit
AI is human - every algorithm connects to the person who built it
Not a career pipeline - awareness, not employment preparation
Readiness not age - levels are competency-based
Mistakes are the method - debugging and failure are curriculum

Pixel Blockers Placement Assessment
Tests Nano Tots concepts. Concept-intensive, not curriculum-intensive. Any child can take it at any time - no tutor needed. Output is a placement recommendation, never a score or grade.
?
How to use: Read questions aloud or let child read independently. When done, match answers to the Placement Guide below. Output is always a recommendation - never a score.
Q1. Look at this sequence: star moon star moon star - What comes next?
Pattern Recognition
If child answers moon - strong Nano Tots foundation
Q2. A recipe says: mix flour, add eggs, bake. What happens if you bake first?
Sequencing - Order Matters
Looking for understanding that order changes the outcome
Q3. What is the difference between a computer and a human? Name one thing a human can do that a computer cannot.
Human vs Machine
Accept any thoughtful answer - feelings, creativity, making decisions without instructions
Q4. You want to teach a robot to make a sandwich. What is the very first instruction?
Algorithm Thinking
Looking for a concrete first step, not a general description
Q5. If you gave a robot the wrong instruction, what would happen? And how would you fix it?
Debugging Awareness
Both parts matter: what goes wrong AND that it can be fixed
Q6. Have you ever used ScratchJr, Scratch, or told a voice assistant to do something?
Prior Experience
Any yes = some prior experience. Not required for entry.
Q7. Which is a pattern? (A) Red Blue Red Blue Red (B) Cat Dog Fish Tree
Visual Pattern Recognition
Answer A - looking for confidence, not just the right answer
Q8. Can a computer feel happy? Why or why not?
AI Awareness
No wrong answer - looking for thoughtful reasoning about what makes humans different
Placement Guide
6-8 confident answers - Ready for Minecrafters. Consider Pixel Blockers Week 40+ or jump to Minecrafters assessment.
3-5 confident answers - Start Pixel Blockers. Prior experience helps identify the right week (typically Week 10-20).
0-2 confident answers - Start at Nano Tots. This is not a failure - it is the right starting point.
