The Learning Journey

Not a ladder. A journey.

Children enter at the level that matches their readiness — not their age. Age suggestions are guides, not gates.

🔬
Nano Tots
6 and above

The tiny invisible science behind AI. Patterns, sequences, ScratchJr stories.

🧩
Pixel Blockers
7 and above

How parts make a picture. Scratch games, logic, cybersecurity, first AI concepts.

⛏️
Minecrafters
8 and above

Build and survive in AI. Real AI tools, training data, bias, ethics, design thinking.

🦄
Unicorns
9 and above

Create from pure imagination. Complex AI projects, investigation, capstone, graduation.

💡 Age is a guide, not a gate. A curious child who is ready can move through levels faster. A child new to computers starts at Nano Tots regardless of age. Use our Find My Level quiz to find the perfect starting point.

Level Details

What happens at each level?

High-level themes and what your child will experience — no curriculum jargon.

🔬
Level 0 · Entry Level
Nano Tots
🌱 6 and above · No experience needed
View Full Curriculum →

The tiny invisible science behind AI — patterns, data, signals, and the first ideas of how computers think.

What they explore

  • Patterns in nature, music, and numbers
  • How machines follow instructions
  • Sorting and classification
  • Cause and effect in technology
  • Computers as human-made tools

What they create

  • ScratchJr animated stories
  • Original pattern designs
  • Step-by-step algorithms
  • Debugging challenges
  • Bee-Bot adventure maps
No prior knowledge 50 weeks Open entry ScratchJr Unplugged activities
🧩
Level 1
Pixel Blockers
🧩 7 and above · Builds on Nano Tots
View Full Curriculum →

How parts make a picture — the foundations of computing, game design, logic, and first encounters with AI concepts.

What they explore

  • Full Scratch — sprites, events, loops
  • Game design: score, lives, timer, levels
  • Nested logic and boolean thinking
  • Cybersecurity and online safety
  • What AI is — concepts only, no tools yet

What they create

  • Complete playable games
  • Interactive stories
  • Sound-designed animations
  • A personal cybersecurity guide
  • An AI-inspired Scratch project
Scratch Game design 50 weeks 10 pioneer stories Assessment available
⛏️
Level 2
Minecrafters
⛏️ 8 and above · Builds on Pixel Blockers
View Full Curriculum →

How to build and survive in AI — this is where AI becomes real. Children train actual models, experience bias, and build things that matter.

What they explore

  • Teachable Machine — image, sound, pose AI
  • ML4Kids — text AI and NLP basics
  • Training data, bias, and fairness
  • AI in medicine, environment, creativity
  • Human-centred design thinking

What they create

  • Real trained AI models
  • AI-powered Scratch applications
  • A personal AI ethics framework
  • An AI capstone project
  • A human-centred design prototype
Teachable Machine ML4Kids 50 weeks 🇨🇦 Canadian AI Assessment available
🦄
Level 3 · Graduation
Unicorns
🦄 9 and above · Builds on Minecrafters
View Full Curriculum →

Creating from pure imagination — the graduation level. Children build ambitious AI projects, investigate real questions, and graduate as Young AI Pioneers.

What they explore

  • Complex multi-tool AI projects
  • Scientific investigation of AI questions
  • AI and the world — rights, planet, fairness
  • Capstone project — build something real
  • AI advocacy and civic voice

What they create

  • A gesture, voice, or text-controlled AI project
  • An AI science fair investigation
  • A letter to a real decision-maker
  • A capstone project for a real audience
  • A 4-level portfolio — their whole journey
